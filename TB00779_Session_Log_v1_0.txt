1. THE HARNESS  [scripts/tb00779_exit_sweep.py, Code f7fc2cc]
   Entry held FIXED = the live-bot-style EMA-12/26 cross (the crossover EVENT), plus a 20-bar momentum
   cross as a robustness check. Swept: STOP SIZE in ATR multiples [0.5, 1, 1.5, 2, 3, 5] (ATR = the
   standard "real price swing" measure; 0.5x ~ the jitter-tight stop the live bot uses) x EXIT POLICY
   {hold = no target, exit on EMA reversal/stop | tp1.5R/tp2R/tp3R = take FULL profit at the target |
   scale3R = bank HALF at +3R, run the rest = Bill's "take SOME profit"} across SPEEDS {5m,15m,30m,1h,4h,1d}
   from Kraken (~720 bars each). Everything measured in R (1R = stop distance = "for each $1 risked"), so
   the 1:1.5 goal is directly readable. Nested IS(0-0.5)/OOS(0.5-1.0) + per-direction + era stability +
   block-bootstrap + fees (taker x2 + short open) + per-bar short rollover. 12 grids total.

2. Q2 - STOP SIZE  [THE DECISIVE FINDING]
   On EVERY timeframe and BOTH entries, OOS E[R] improves MONOTONICALLY as the stop WIDENS. Example (5m
   emacross): 0.5x=-5.32R, 1x=-2.88, 1.5x=-2.00, 2x=-1.51, 3x=-1.02, 5x=-0.63. Same shape on all 12 grids.
   MECHANISM: a tight stop is tagged constantly by noise (each tag = a full -1R + fees), and when the stop
   distance R is tiny the fee fraction (fees/R) is huge - so tight stops bleed on both stop-outs and fee
   churn. CONCLUSION: the tight jitter stop IS the bleed; wider is strictly better. The live bot's ~0.4%
   stop sits well under 1x ATR = squarely in the WORST zone. This empirically confirms Bill's hypothesis
   and the TB00774 entry-economics audit.

3. Q3 - SPEED
   Grids get steadily LESS negative as the speed SLOWS. 5m = disaster (-5R range). 15m/30m bad. 1h better.
   4h = the FIRST timeframe where positive cells appear (momentum/hold/wide-stop +0.10 to +0.14R; emacross
   5x +0.04R). 1d = small/mixed. CONCLUSION: slower trades this strategy better; the 5m the live bot runs
   on is the WORST possible speed; 4h is the sweet spot. CAVEAT: Kraken serves ~720 bars/timeframe, so 5m
   spans ~2.5 days (one regime) vs 1d ~2 years - shorter speeds are more regime-fragile here; the growing
   VPS 5m corpus will give a longer 5m window later.

4. Q1 - TAKE PROFIT vs HOLD-AND-HOPE
   Roughly a WASH. Within a row, "hold" (run to reversal) is generally as good or BETTER than a fixed
   take-profit, especially at faster speeds (e.g. 4h momentum: hold +0.139R at 1x beats every TP in that
   row). Fixed TPs cap the winners, which on a trend-following entry costs about what it saves. scale3R
   (take half, run the rest) is middle-of-pack. CONCLUSION: mechanically "taking some profit" does NOT
   clearly beat holding/letting winners run; the instinct to run winners is mildly right.

5. THE HONEST META (carried forward)
   Even at the BEST exit (wide stop) + BEST speed (4h) + best policy, the mechanical EMA-cross/momentum
   ENTRIES only reach break-even to slightly positive - NOT a robust 1:1.5, and nothing passed the strict
   both-halves nested-validation gate. So the exit+speed fixes STOP THE BLEED (turn -5R into ~break-even)
   but do NOT by themselves manufacture the edge. The 1:1.5 edge still needs ENTRY quality, which the whole
   hunt has not cracked (mechanical entries keep yielding regime artifacts). BUT stopping the bleed is huge:
   the live bot today is in the -5R zone (tight stop on 5m); two changes (widen the stop, slow the speed)
   move it to a fair fight.
