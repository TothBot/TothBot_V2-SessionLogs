5. ENGINEERING CHOICES (per the NSI, NOT Bill rulings - documented here)
   (1) The after_batch pump runs on the PRIVATE loop only (not the public shards): the private socket is
       guaranteed bound when not reconnecting (the transmitter targets it), and the private loop pumps
       every tick (<= tick_interval) even on a recv timeout, so a public-ticker/instrument-enqueued intent
       drains within ~1 tick. Pumping from a public shard would risk dispatching over an unbound/mid-
       reconnect private transmitter (public + private have SEPARATE reconnect coordinators) - avoided.
   (2) The C-1 default reject probe POLLS the order-response registry (rejected/accepted, short-circuit on
       either) up to cancel_timeout_window - the consistent sibling of _default_cancel_ack_wait. The pre-
       existing TB00763 happy-path dispatch tests relied on the old instant-False stub; they now inject an
       "accepted" stub (a _never_reject default in _live_manager, the analogue of injecting _always_ack),
       preserving their intent. A genuine response in live resolves the probe in ms (no full-window wait).
   (3) The gap-close uses QueryOrders (REST-QOI) for the actual fill (FEE-CALC-006) on the async WS-REC-004
       restore path; the sync ExecutionsIngest snapshot path emits the degraded estimate (it cannot await
       REST). Idempotency (restore_from_snapshot removes the symbol) prevents a double TRADE_CLOSE.
   (4) ALL new wiring into a possibly-lightweight wm stand-in is getattr-guarded (matching operational.py's
       set_ciats_exit_sinks pattern) so the operational/assemble tests' _FakeWM / _LiveWM are untouched.
