GATE 5 — SUBSUMED FINDINGS PLAIN-LANGUAGE EXPLANATION
WHY 9 FINDINGS REQUIRE NO SEPARATE DISPOSITION
============================================================

============================================================
1. WHAT GATE 5 IS ASKING
============================================================

The Step 3 close-gate package collected 24 findings
from the 13 module-pair audits (TB00194 through
TB00206). Most of those findings need a decision.
Gate 1 handles the 17 diagram amendments. Gate 2
handles the 6 hard-rule registration occurrences.
Gate 3 handles the 17-spec currency-lag cluster.
Gate 4 handles the 8 standalone findings.

That leaves 9 findings in Gate 5. These 9 are
NOT separate decisions. Each one is already
covered by something else Bill is ratifying in
Gates 1 through 4. Asking Bill to ratify them
again would be redundant — and worse, it could
introduce inconsistency if Bill ratifies the
parent gate one way and the subsumed finding
another way.

The job of this analysis is to walk through each
of the 9 findings, explain in plain English what
the finding actually is, name the gate that
absorbs it, show why no separate action is
required, and confirm that no finding actually
needs its own discrete disposition after all.

If any of the 9 findings turn out to need their
own disposition, this analysis re-classifies them
as Gate 4 candidates and surfaces them for Bill
ratification.

The 9 findings:

  F-13  Diagram D2 R2 G6 cell still references a
        retired parameter.

  F-08  Risk Engine coding spec and its four AA=06
        parents are all out of date with respect
        to the v2_29 framework rewrite.

  F-11  Regime Engine coding spec is out of date
        with respect to six framework amendment
        classes.

  F-17  Operations Infrastructure parent spec is
        out of date with respect to 10 framework
        amendment classes — the largest single
        currency-lag-driven revision in Step 3.

  F-21  Startup/Shutdown procedure spec pre-dates
        the entire paper-as-master cascade and
        contains zero paper-mode prose — the
        largest single-spec lag in project history.

  F-22  Diagram AR-056 reconnect-attempt-count
        prose lags the current canonical values.

  F-23  Diagram AR-049 warm-up enumeration prose
        lags the current canonical separation
        between per-pair warm-up and regime-engine
        warm-up.

  F-10  Regime Engine coding spec cites three
        other documents at stale version numbers.

  F-19  75 governance-rule tokens defined in the
        Startup/Shutdown procedure spec are
        completely absent from the architecture
        diagram.

2.1 F-13 — D2 R2 G6 CELL CITES RETIRED PARAMETER
------------------------------------------------------------

WHAT THE FINDING IS.

Cell G6 in diagram D2 revision 2 lists the
parameters its module consumes. One of those
parameters is "per_trade_pct" — a parameter that
the v2_29 amendment formally retired and replaced
with two CIATS-owned parameters (per_trade_size_
usd starting at the Kraken per-pair minimum, and
per_trade_ceiling_pct starting at 25% of the
module wallet). The cell still names the retired
parameter as a current consume.

WHICH GATE ABSORBS IT.

Gate 4, finding F-12. F-12 already specifies a
cell-prose update for D2 R2 G6 that replaces the
"per_trade_pct" reference with the two
replacement parameters. The text Bill ratifies at
F-12 makes the F-13 problem disappear in the
same edit.

WHY NO SEPARATE ACTION IS REQUIRED.

The G6 cell is touched once when AMENDMENT-15
is authored after Gate 4 ratification. That one
edit fixes both findings simultaneously. Treating
F-13 as a separate decision would invite a
double-edit (one edit to fix F-12, a second edit
to fix F-13) which is two passes on the same
cell — exactly the rework the Gate 4 decision
criterion is designed to prevent.

CONFIRMATION.

F-13 is correctly subsumed under F-12. No
discrete disposition required.

2.2 F-08 — 7th CONSECUTIVE CURRENCY-LAG (RISK
            ENGINE + 4 AA=06 PARENTS)
------------------------------------------------------------

WHAT THE FINDING IS.

Five specifications in the Risk family are out of
date relative to the v2_29 amendment:

  - 1011011 (Risk Engine coding spec).
  - 0611001 (Position Sizing Rules).
  - 0611002 (Drawdown and Halt Rules).
  - 0611003 (Concentration and Exposure Rules).
  - 0611004 (Safety Parameter Framework).

Across these five specs, seven framework
amendment classes have not been propagated:

  (A) Parallel-module framework (Long_Module +
      Short_Module + future modules).
  (B) Per-module CIATS instances.
  (C) Per-wallet drawdown halts (replacing per-
      portfolio).
  (D) The R:R minimum as a SACRED FLOOR rather
      than a fixed ratio.
  (E) The R:R-driven sizing curve.
  (F) Retired parameters (tradeable_pct,
      per_trade_pct, max_concurrent, the
      hardcoded Half-Kelly transition at 200
      trades).
  (G) The per-module $5,000 paper-trading wallet
      starting balance.

The Risk Engine coding spec also still describes
the v2_28 architecture in its §1 PURPOSE, and
the 0611004 Safety Parameter Framework still
cites the retired parameters as canonical in its
§6.1 Parameter Registry.

WHICH GATE ABSORBS IT.

Gate 3. The currency-lag reconciliation strategy
ratified at Gate 3 (the hybrid phased + governance-
layer fix) puts coding specifications in Phase 1
of the post-ratification cleanup, with the AA=06
risk parents following in their own phase
according to the priority ordering. All five
specs in F-08 are inside Gate 3's scope.

WHY NO SEPARATE ACTION IS REQUIRED.

Gate 3 specifies the WHOLE strategy for closing
the 17-spec currency-lag cluster — including
which specs get fixed in which phase, what the
governance-layer fix looks like, and how to
prevent future lag from accumulating. F-08 is one
of the input findings that produced the 17-spec
count. Asking Bill to ratify F-08 separately
would be asking the same question Gate 3 already
asked.

CONFIRMATION.

F-08 is correctly subsumed under Gate 3. No
discrete disposition required.

The Startup/Shutdown procedure specification
(0211003 dv1_0, dated 04-05-2026) pre-dates the
entire paper-as-master cascade that landed
across April and early May 2026. Specifically,
it pre-dates:

  - 0500000 dv1_24+ §12 (the paper-mode
    dispatch boundary in the architecture
    diagram).
  - TB00000 v2_18+ §4.4 PA-001 through PA-010
    (the paper-as-master absolute standing
    rules).
  - 0211005 dv1_3 PT-MODE-001 through PT-MODE-
    007 (paper-trading operations procedure).

As a result, 0211003 §3 through §8 contain ZERO
paper-mode prose. Ten distinct paper-mode
amendment classes are missing — pair-state
machine paper-mode awareness, startup-step
paper-mode gates, portfolio-baseline paper
synthetic-ledger branch, mid-session reconnection
paper-mode invariants, hard-rule paper-mode
bindings, related-documents currency, alert-
recipient plural form, Call C interval=1440
removal, reconnect-attempt-count refresh per
OI-020, and READY-criterion enumeration per
VD-WD-004 sub-conditions.

F-21 is the LARGEST single-spec lag in project
history — a complete paper-mode rewrite of one
procedure document.

WHICH GATE ABSORBS IT.

Gate 3. 0211003 sits in the AA=02 operational
procedure phase of the Gate 3 phased
reconciliation.

WHY NO SEPARATE ACTION IS REQUIRED.

The Gate 3 strategy already accounts for the
existence of large single-spec lags. The phased
approach is designed precisely so that large
specs get the attention they need without
blocking smaller ones. 0211003 will get its own
multi-session amendment cycle inside Phase 2 of
Gate 3. F-21 is the input finding that surfaced
the need for that cycle.

  - The READY-criterion enumeration subset

The Phase 2 0211003 amendment will be authored

2.6 F-22 — DIAGRAM AR-056 RECONNECT-ATTEMPT-COUNT
            CURRENCY-LAG
------------------------------------------------------------

WHAT THE FINDING IS.

The architecture diagram (0500000 dv1_46)
contains a prose block for AR-056 (the mid-
session WebSocket reconnect rule). The AR-056
prose still describes the pre-OI-020 reconnect
schedule: "10 attempts over 120 seconds." The
current canonical schedule lives in 1011014 dv1_8
SS-RECON-015 and 1011002 dv1_26 WM-RECONNECT-002
and -017: "20 total attempts spanning
approximately 481 seconds (~8 minutes)."

The diagram prose has not been updated since the
OI-020 amendment changed the schedule.

WHICH GATE ABSORBS IT.

Gate 3. This is a currency-lag finding even
though the lagging document IS the architecture
diagram. The diagram-prose-update lives in the
same Gate 3 phased reconciliation strategy as the
spec-prose updates — likely sequenced near the
end of the strategy when all dependent specs have
already been brought current, so the diagram
update can propagate the post-Phase-1 + Phase-2
canonical content.

WHY NO SEPARATE ACTION IS REQUIRED.

The diagrams-as-canonical principle (§4.5
DD-001 through DD-011) means the diagram must
agree with the canonical content. When two
canonical sources agree (1011014 + 1011002 both
specify "20 attempts over ~481 seconds") and the
diagram disagrees, the diagram prose is the
defect — not the canonical sources. The fix is
mechanical (substitute the current values into
the diagram prose). Gate 3's hybrid strategy
puts this kind of mechanical refresh into its
sequenced phase without separate ratification.

A NOTE ON DIAGRAM-INTERNAL PROSE LAG.

F-22 (and F-23 below) are slightly unusual
within the currency-lag cluster because the
lagging document is the architecture diagram
itself rather than a downstream spec. This does
not change the disposition — both still get
fixed under Gate 3 — but it does illustrate that
even canonical artifacts can fall behind when
amendment cycles refresh their downstream
descendants first.

CONFIRMATION.

F-22 is correctly subsumed under Gate 3. No
discrete disposition required.

2.7 F-23 — DIAGRAM AR-049 CALL C INTERVAL=1440
            CURRENCY-LAG
------------------------------------------------------------

WHAT THE FINDING IS.

The architecture diagram (0500000 dv1_46)
contains a prose block for AR-049 (the warm-up
rule). Step (8) of that prose still enumerates
three REST OHLC warm-up calls together: "REST
warm-up GetOHLCData (AR-044: interval=5, 60,
1440)."

The current canonical separation, per 1011014
dv1_8 §6 Step 8 SS-WARMUP-003 NOTE, is:

  - Per-pair warm-up uses Call A (interval=5)
    and Call B (interval=60) only.
  - Call C (interval=1440, the daily candle for
    regime classification) is NOT part of per-
    pair warm-up. It is owned by mod:Regime_
    Engine independently and operates at the
    portfolio level, not per pair.

The diagram prose collapses the three calls back
together as if they were a single per-pair
operation.

WHICH GATE ABSORBS IT.

Gate 3. Same class as F-22 — diagram-prose
currency-lag against a canonical source in the
coding-spec layer.

WHY NO SEPARATE ACTION IS REQUIRED.

The fix is mechanical (split the three-call
enumeration into per-pair Calls A + B plus a
separate regime-engine-owned Call C). The
canonical source is unambiguous. Gate 3's
sequencing handles this kind of diagram refresh
without separate ratification.

CONFIRMATION.

F-23 is correctly subsumed under Gate 3. No
discrete disposition required.

2.9 F-19 — 75 STARTUP/SHUTDOWN RULE TOKENS ABSENT
            FROM DIAGRAM
------------------------------------------------------------

WHAT THE FINDING IS.

The Startup/Shutdown procedure spec (0211003)
defines 75 governance-identifier-referenced
rules grouped into seven categories:

  - SS-PRE-* (pre-startup conditions).
  - SS-STARTUP-* (startup sequence steps).
  - SS-WARMUP-* (indicator warm-up).
  - SS-PAPER-* (paper-trading-mode behaviors).
  - SS-RECON-* (mid-session reconnection).
  - SS-WIRE-* (wiring constraint rules).
  - HR-SS-* (hard rules).

Zero of those 75 tokens currently appear in any
governs list, prose paragraph, or architectural
reasoning entry in the architecture diagram
(0500000 dv1_46). The Startup_Sequence module's
governs list in the diagram is essentially empty
relative to the 75 rules it actually owns.

This is the LARGEST single-spec cluster C5
finding across all 12 module audits.

WHICH GATE ABSORBS IT.

Gate 1, AMENDMENT-17 specifically. AMENDMENT-17
in the close-gate package §2 enumeration is:
"D7 R3 governs ADD SS ~120 items (LARGEST
single-spec amendment)." When Bill ratifies
AMENDMENT-17 at the Gate 1 ratification step,
the 75 rule tokens (plus related architectural-
reasoning entries and bindings, bringing the
count toward 120) get registered in the
diagram's governs list for the Startup Sequence
module.

WHY NO SEPARATE ACTION IS REQUIRED.

F-19 is the input finding that surfaced
AMENDMENT-17. Ratifying AMENDMENT-17 IS the
disposition for F-19. The TB00208 AMENDMENT-17
plain-language explanation document already
walks through what 0211003 contains, what is
missing from the diagram, why fixing it matters,
and what the fix is. That document is the
detailed explanation backing Bill's Gate 1
ratification decision for AMENDMENT-17.

CONFIRMATION.

F-19 is closed at AMENDMENT-17 ratification
(Gate 1). No separate disposition required.
