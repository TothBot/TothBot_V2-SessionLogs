3.3 WEIGHTED AVERAGE — FOUND BUT NOT AS BILL DESCRIBED.

Two weighted-average computations exist in legacy; neither
matches "weighted average across module scores":

  (a) Within-module weighted product (0711001 §6):
      signal_score = DPS_Edge_Factor × Momentum_Alignment_Factor.
      This is a PRODUCT of two factors, not a weighted
      average. Option B reduction made this single-factor
      effectively.

  (b) Cross-position portfolio score (0731002 §3.3):
      Portfolio_Impact_Score = weighted average of
      Signed_Correlation(candidate, existing_i) across
      all active positions. This is a Selection Controller
      computation INSIDE Gate 2 (Signed Correlation Gate)
      for portfolio risk evaluation. It is NOT a weighted
      average of cross-module scores.

  No weighted average of cross-module scores exists in
  legacy V1.

3.4 "SELECTION MODULE PICKS NEXT PAIR FROM QUEUE" —
    NOT FOUND AS DESCRIBED IN LEGACY.

Selection Controller does NOT rank by score and pick
highest. Per 0731001 §4.2: processes per-arrival (FIFO);
not priority-sorted. signal_score feeds Gate 6 as a
pass/fail threshold check (against MIN_SIGNAL_SCORE), not
as ranking input. Producer-consumer FIFO, not priority
queue.

============================================================

7.1 BILL DIRECTIVE AT CHAT TURN 6.

"Is this even necessary. Both Longs and Shorts have their

7.2 FP/DP REASSESSMENT.

V1 paradigm required ranking because three conditions
were stacked:
  (1) max_concurrent = 20 was a HARD RULE (not CIATS-owned).
  (2) Single capital pool (Longs and Shorts competed for
      same money).
  (3) Universe was large enough that simultaneous SSS PASS
      signals could exceed 20.

Given those three conditions, when passing signals exceeded
slot capacity, choosing was REQUIRED. Ranking by score was
the choosing mechanism.

V2 paradigm removes those conditions:
  (1) Per TB00000 v2_28 §9.17 V2 parameter philosophy:
      max_concurrent is CIATS-owned starting value, not
      hard rule. Only net 1:1.5 R:R is sacred.
  (2) Bill stated intent (and currently TB00177 NSI §2.3
      ITEM 1 fork): separate Long and Short capital pools
      via per-side capacity cap split. Eliminates cross-
      direction competition.
  (3) Within a direction, capital arithmetic produces
      natural cap: tradeable_pct × per_trade_pct (currently
      50% × 5% = 10 positions per direction by arithmetic).

7.3 EDGE CASE HANDLING WITHOUT RANKING.

When more passing signals exist than direction-pool
capacity allows on a single candle close (rare per V1
simulation context — N=594,550 LONG observations):
  - FIFO (arrival order) — simplest, deterministic
  - Skip remaining; re-evaluate on next candle close
    5 minutes later
  - Tighten gates via CIATS if too much passes

None require a scoring engine. Gates are the quality
filter.
