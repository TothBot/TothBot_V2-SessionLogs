GATE 4 — FIRST-PRINCIPLES / DATA-POINT ANALYSIS
PER-FINDING DISPOSITIONS (8 DISCRETE FINDINGS)
DEFINITIVE RECOMMENDATIONS
============================================================

============================================================
1. WHAT GATE 4 IS ASKING
============================================================

Gates 1 through 3 in the close-gate package handle
issues that affect many places at once (the 17
diagram amendments, the 6 hard-rule registration
occurrences, the 17-spec currency-lag cluster).
Gate 4 is different. Gate 4 collects 8 individual
findings that each need their own decision because
each one stands alone — no joint resolution
applies, no universal pattern fits.

Each of the 8 findings has its own facts, its own
options, and its own correct answer. The job of
this analysis is to walk through each finding,
explain in plain English what the problem actually
is, weigh the options against the decision
criterion, and recommend a specific disposition.

The decision criterion is uniform across all eight
findings: pick the option that produces the fewest
errors and the least redo work AND keeps the
canonical architecture diagram telling the truth
about how the system works.

The eight findings:

  F-01  When the trading bot tries to exit a
        position but the remaining amount is below
        Kraken's minimum order size, two
        specifications disagree on whether CIATS
        (the continuous improvement engine) should
        be told about the event.

  F-03  Two specifications give two different names
        to the same internal data structure that
        tracks how long pairs must wait after an
        exit before being eligible again. The
        architecture diagram uses one of the names.

  F-04  Two specifications give two different
        function signatures to the callback that
        fires when a position closes. One uses a
        boolean (win or loss). The other uses an
        enum (the actual exit reason).

  F-07 + F-15  Two specifications cite incomplete
        parent chains. The Risk Engine coding spec
        cites three of its four AA=06 parent specs
        instead of all four. The Operations
        Infrastructure parent spec is not cited
        anywhere in the architecture diagram even
        though VPS Deployment is built from it.

  F-12  Two cells in diagram D2 (the per-pair
        pipeline diagram) contain prose that
        predates the project's adoption of the
        directional-cascade regime framework and
        the v2_29 parameter-registry rewrite.

  F-16  Four places in the Operations
        Infrastructure spec route alerts to a
        singular mailbox name that does not exist
        at the mail provider. Alerts sent to that
        address bounce. The plural name (which is
        what actually works) has already been
        adopted everywhere else.

  F-18  The Operations Infrastructure spec cites
        four other documents by version number,
        and three of those version numbers are
        stale.

  F-24  The VPS Deployment coding spec defines the
        condition under which the bot announces
        itself READY to the operating system, and
        two parts of that condition are
        inconsistent with three canonical sources.

============================================================
2. DECISION CRITERION
============================================================

Each finding's recommendation is judged against
the same five criteria:

  (i)   Correctness against the diagrams-as-
        canonical principle. The architecture
        diagram (0500000) is the authoritative
        source. When prose, code, or specs
        contradict the diagram, the diagram
        governs.

  (ii)  Architectural-spec precedence. When an
        AA=08 architectural specification and an
        AA=10 coding specification disagree, the
        AA=08 spec governs unless the AA=10 spec
        documents an explicit, ratified
        amendment.

  (iii) Loss prevention. Where a choice affects
        the bot's ability to detect or prevent
        loss, the loss-prevention path wins. This
        is the primary mission.

  (iv)  Information richness for CIATS. CIATS
        improves the system by analyzing data.
        More information per trade outcome means
        CIATS can detect more problems sooner.
        Where a choice affects what information
        CIATS receives, the richer path wins
        unless it costs loss-prevention.

  (v)   Fewest errors and least rework. Pick the
        option that closes the finding on the
        first pass with no follow-up cycle.

Each per-finding analysis below walks through
these criteria explicitly.

3.1 F-01 — PARTIAL-FILL-BELOW-MINIMUM CIATS CONTRADICTION
------------------------------------------------------------

WHAT THE FINDING IS.

The Long Module specification (0811001) describes
what happens when the bot tries to scale out of a
position but the remaining amount is below Kraken's
minimum order size. The spec says: dispatch a market
sell immediately, log the event as
PARTIAL_FILL_BELOW_MINIMUM, and fire the CIATS
Trade Outcome Bus so CIATS sees the event.

The Long Module coding specification (1011008) says
the opposite: do not fire CIATS for below-minimum
exits, because "insufficient trade data."

The two documents directly contradict each other.
Neither contradiction has diagram authority backing
(the architecture diagram doesn't currently encode
either form).

OPTION A — FIRE CIATS on below-minimum partial
fills.

OPTION B — Do not fire CIATS on below-minimum
partial fills.

ANALYSIS.

Criterion (i) diagrams-canonical: neither option
is currently in the diagram. Whichever option wins
becomes the canonical form and gets registered in
the diagram. Both options score equal on this
criterion.

Criterion (ii) AA=08 over AA=10: the AA=08
architectural specification (0811001) says FIRE
CIATS. The AA=10 coding spec says don't. By
default AA=08 governs. Option A wins on this
criterion unless an explicit, ratified amendment
overrides — and no such amendment exists. The
coding spec's "insufficient trade data" reasoning
is not documented as an origin-investigated
constraint per RR-SCOPE-009 (no constraint removal
without origin investigation); it appears to be
post-hoc reasoning.

Criterion (iii) loss prevention: a partial fill
that drops below minimum is a real fill that
produced real P&L. The bot has spent real capital
(or real synthetic paper capital). Hiding that
event from CIATS hides loss data. If a particular
pair or regime regularly produces
below-minimum-residual exits, CIATS needs to see
that pattern. The "insufficient trade data"
argument is weak because below-minimum events are
themselves a data class CIATS should learn about —
not noise to be discarded.

Criterion (iv) information richness: firing CIATS
gives CIATS more data. Suppressing CIATS gives
CIATS less data. CIATS is designed to handle
small-sample data (EWMA is valid at 50 candles for
monitoring; CUSUM detects shifts on a few
observations). The threshold for parameter-change
decisions is 200 trades, not 200 below-minimum
events. Adding below-minimum events to the trade
pool moves the pool toward 200 faster — which
serves loss prevention by making CIATS effective
sooner.

Criterion (v) rework cost: either option needs
one document amendment and one diagram update.
Option A amends 1011008 to remove the suppression
rule. Option B amends 0811001 to remove the
fire-CIATS instruction. Both are single-pass
fixes. Tie on rework cost.

Three of the five criteria point to Option A
(AA=08 precedence, loss prevention, information
richness). Two are ties. No criterion points to
Option B.

RECOMMENDATION.

Option A — FIRE CIATS on partial fills that drop
below minimum. Specifically:

  - 0811001 §5.5 LM-019(2) text retained
    unchanged (this is now the canonical form).
  - 1011008 §8 LM-PARTIAL-BELOW-MIN-001 amended
    to align: remove the "no CIATS" rule;
    replace with "fire CIATS Trade Outcome Bus
    with event tag PARTIAL_FILL_BELOW_MINIMUM."
  - Architecture diagram registers a new rule
    token (rule:LM-PARTIAL-BELOW-MIN-FIRES-CIATS)
    in the Long Module governs list at the next
    diagram revision.

WHY THIS DECISION IS DERIVABLE WITHOUT BILL
JUDGMENT.

AA=08-over-AA=10 precedence is a project standing
rule. Loss prevention is the project's primary
mission. CIATS data sufficiency is a CIATS design
property, not a Bill business choice. The
recommendation follows from these existing
canonical sources without Bill judgment. Per
RR-SCOPE-005 (derivable-decision execution),
Option A is presented as the recommendation
without three-option enumeration.

3.2 F-03 — STATE-DICTIONARY NAMING MISMATCH
------------------------------------------------------------

WHAT THE FINDING IS.

The Selection Controller manages an internal data
structure that records when each trading pair
finished its most recent exit, so the pair can be
kept out of selection for a cooldown period
before becoming eligible again.

Three places name this data structure:

  - The architectural specification (0811002 §6.1
    SC-019) calls it "exit_cooldown_log."
  - The coding specification (1011009 §4
    SC-STATE-001) calls it "cooldown_registry."
  - The architecture diagram (0500000 D6 R4 +
    D3 R2) calls it "exit_cooldown_log."

The architectural spec and the diagram agree on
"exit_cooldown_log." Only the coding spec is the
outlier.

OPTION X — Align the coding spec name to match
the architectural spec and the diagram. Rename
"cooldown_registry" to "exit_cooldown_log" in
1011009.

OPTION Y — Invert the alignment. Rename
"exit_cooldown_log" to "cooldown_registry" in
the architectural spec and in the diagram.

ANALYSIS.

Criterion (i) diagrams-canonical: the diagram
currently uses "exit_cooldown_log." Option X
preserves the diagram. Option Y requires a
diagram amendment (changing a cell text string
in D6 R4 and D3 R2), which is real work and
introduces SVG-byte-change risk for those two
cells. Option X scores higher.

Criterion (ii) AA=08 over AA=10: AA=08 says
"exit_cooldown_log." AA=10 says
"cooldown_registry." AA=08 governs. Option X
matches AA=08. Option Y inverts the precedence
rule — would require explicit Bill override of
the precedence rule, which is not in evidence.
Option X scores higher.

Criterion (iii) loss prevention: this is a
naming choice with zero behavioral impact on
loss-prevention rules. Tie.

Criterion (iv) information richness: zero impact.
Tie.

Criterion (v) rework cost: Option X amends one
document (1011009) in one place — change the
identifier string and any references to it.
Option Y amends two architectural artifacts
(0811002 and 0500000 dv1_46) plus all downstream
references — at least 3-4 places, with diagram
amendment overhead. Option X is dramatically
cheaper.

Three criteria favor Option X. Two are ties.
None favors Option Y.

RECOMMENDATION.

Option X — Align the coding spec to the
architectural spec and the diagram. Specifically:

  - 1011009 §4 SC-STATE-001 amended to rename
    the data structure from "cooldown_registry"
    to "exit_cooldown_log."
  - All other references in 1011009 to
    "cooldown_registry" updated to
    "exit_cooldown_log" (search-and-replace
    scope).
  - 0811002 §6.1 SC-019 unchanged (already
    canonical).
  - Diagram D6 R4 + D3 R2 unchanged (already
    canonical).

WHY THIS DECISION IS DERIVABLE WITHOUT BILL
JUDGMENT.

The AA=08-over-AA=10 precedence rule plus the
diagrams-as-canonical principle make Option X
the mechanical outcome. No Bill business
judgment is required.

3.3 F-04 — on_position_closed CALLBACK SIGNATURE
            MISMATCH
------------------------------------------------------------

WHAT THE FINDING IS.

When a position closes in the trading system, the
Exit Controller fires a callback into the
Selection Controller so the Selection Controller
can update its internal state (mark the pair as
cooled-down, etc.). Two specifications give two
different function signatures for this callback:

  - The coding spec (1011009 SC-STATE-004) uses:
    on_position_closed(symbol, was_win: bool).
    The Exit Controller passes a boolean
    indicating win or loss.

  - The architectural spec (0811002 §6.2 SC-024)
    uses:
    on_position_closed(symbol, exit_reason: enum).
    The Exit Controller passes an enum value
    naming the exit type (signal-based exit,
    take-profit, MAE breach, emergency stop-loss,
    etc.).

OPTION X — boolean signature, win/loss
classification performed inside the Exit
Controller before the callback fires.

OPTION Y — enum signature, exit-reason
classification performed in the Exit Controller
and passed through; Selection Controller derives
win/loss locally if needed.

ANALYSIS.

Criterion (i) diagrams-canonical: the
architecture diagram does not currently encode
the callback signature in any cell text. The
diagram is neutral on signature form. Tie on
diagram-correctness.

Criterion (ii) AA=08 over AA=10: AA=08 says enum.
AA=10 says boolean. AA=08 governs. Option Y
matches AA=08. Option Y scores higher.

Criterion (iii) loss prevention: an enum carries
strictly more information than a boolean. A
boolean answers one question: did this trade
make money or not. An enum answers two questions:
which of the bot's four exit layers fired
(Layer 1a signal, Layer 1b take-profit, Layer 2
MAE breach, Layer 3 emergency stop), AND
implicitly, what was the outcome. Knowing which
layer fired is a loss-prevention signal — if
Layer 2 MAE breaches start clustering on a
particular regime, that is data CIATS needs to
adjust MAE multiplier. The boolean form discards
this information. Option Y scores higher.

Criterion (iv) information richness for CIATS:
the enum form carries exit-layer information
through to CIATS via downstream logging. The
boolean form loses it (or requires CIATS to
derive it from a separate event source — extra
coupling and extra failure modes). Option Y
scores higher.

Criterion (v) rework cost: either option amends
one document. Option X amends 0811002 to switch
from enum to boolean and to add a paragraph
explaining where exit-reason classification now
lives. Option Y amends 1011009 SC-STATE-004 to
switch from boolean to enum and to add the enum
value list. Both are single-pass fixes. Tie on
rework cost.

Three criteria favor Option Y (AA=08 precedence,
loss prevention via exit-layer information,
information richness for CIATS). Two are ties.
None favors Option X.

RECOMMENDATION.

Option Y — enum signature. Specifically:

  - 1011009 SC-STATE-004 amended to change the
    signature from on_position_closed(symbol,
    was_win: bool) to on_position_closed(symbol,
    exit_reason: enum).
  - The enum value set is the existing Exit
    Controller exit-layer taxonomy
    (SIGNAL_EXIT, TP_HIT, MAE_BREACH,
    EMERGENCY_SL plus any sub-layer values
    already in 0511004 Exit Controller spec).
  - 0811002 §6.2 SC-024 unchanged (already
    canonical).
  - Selection Controller derives win/loss
    locally from the enum when it needs to (a
    one-line helper).

WHY THIS DECISION IS DERIVABLE WITHOUT BILL
JUDGMENT.

AA=08-over-AA=10 precedence plus information-
richness for CIATS plus the empirical observation
that the enum form is strictly more informative
make Option Y the mechanical outcome. No Bill
business judgment is required.

3.4 F-07 + F-15 — PARENT-CHAIN-COMPLETENESS RULE
------------------------------------------------------------

WHAT THE FINDING IS.

These two findings raise the same question from two
different angles.

F-07: The Risk Engine coding spec (1011011 dv1_3
§2 Parent Chain) cites three of its four AA=06
parent specifications: 0611001 Position Sizing
Rules, 0611002 Drawdown and Halt Rules, 0611003
Concentration and Exposure Rules. It does NOT
cite 0611004 Safety_Parameter_Framework. The
coding spec actually USES content from 0611004
(its §5.3 RE-CIATS-001 rule references the
Parameter Store, which is defined in 0611004) —
but doesn't formally cite 0611004 as a parent.

F-15: The Operations Infrastructure spec
(0211002) is the AA=02 parent for the VPS
Deployment coding spec (1011013). 1011013 §2
correctly cites 0211002 as its parent. But
0211002 is not cited anywhere in the
architecture diagram (0500000 dv1_46). The
diagram is silent about the VPS Deployment
module's parent spec.

Both findings are asking the same question: when
a coding spec or architectural artifact has
multiple parent documents, must EVERY parent be
cited, or is "most of them" acceptable?

OPTION (confirm 4-parent canonical requirement)
— Every parent spec that a child document
implements rules from MUST be cited in the
child's parent chain. Missing citations are
defects requiring fix.

OPTION (accept 3-parent) — Citations are
informational; missing one is acceptable if the
omission is benign.

ANALYSIS.

Criterion (i) diagrams-canonical: the
diagrams-as-canonical principle requires that
the canonical architecture diagram tell the truth
about every module's parent chain. A missing
parent citation breaks this — the diagram (or
the linked coding spec) no longer fully reflects
the spec dependency graph. Accept-3-parent
weakens the diagram's truth-telling power.
Confirm-4-parent preserves it. Confirm scores
higher.

Criterion (ii) AA=08 over AA=10: this criterion
doesn't directly apply here — the question is
about parent-citation completeness, not about
AA=08-vs-AA=10 content. Slight edge to confirm
because the missing parent (in F-07) is an AA=06
parent that the AA=10 coding spec is bound to
follow.

Criterion (iii) loss prevention: 0611004
Safety_Parameter_Framework defines the Parameter
Store and the safety rules around CIATS-owned
parameter values. The Risk Engine coding spec
implements rules that depend on the Parameter
Store. If a developer reads 1011011 §2 Parent
Chain and sees only three of four parents, they
might miss a 0611004 safety constraint when
amending the Risk Engine. That is a loss-
prevention risk. Confirm-4-parent eliminates the
risk. Accept-3-parent leaves it open. Confirm
scores higher.

Criterion (iv) information richness: complete
parent chains give downstream readers (and
downstream Claude sessions during cold-load) a
full picture of dependencies. Incomplete chains
hide dependencies. Confirm scores higher.

Criterion (v) rework cost: confirming 4-parent
canonical means amending two documents (1011011
parent chain to add 0611004; 0500000 architecture
prose to add a reference to 0211002 as the
Operations Infrastructure parent for VPS
Deployment). Both are mechanical citation
additions. Accepting 3-parent means accepting
ongoing audit drift — every future audit pass
will surface these as findings again. Confirm is
cheaper over time.

Four of the five criteria favor confirm.
Criterion (ii) is a tie or weak edge for confirm.
None favors accept.

RECOMMENDATION.

Confirm the 4-parent canonical requirement.
Establish parent-chain-completeness as a
documented rule: every coding specification cites
every parent spec whose rules it implements;
every architectural artifact cites every parent
spec whose module it depicts. Specifically:

  - 1011011 §2 Parent Chain amended to add
    0611004 Safety_Parameter_Framework as the
    fourth parent citation. Verify that all
    RE-CIATS-* rules in 1011011 correctly cite
    0611004 content where they reference the
    Parameter Store.
  - 0500000 dv1_46 prose amended to cite 0211002
    Operations_Infrastructure as the AA=02
    parent for the VPS Deployment module
    depicted in the diagram.
  - The parent-chain-completeness rule itself
    formalized at the next 0311002 amendment
    cycle (this is a governance-layer item that
    follows the close-gate ratification and may
    bundle into the DC-PREFLIGHT-NEW per-
    amendment cascade-currency-check from Gate 3).

WHY THIS DECISION IS DERIVABLE WITHOUT BILL
JUDGMENT.

Diagrams-as-canonical + loss-prevention via
dependency-visibility + RR-SCOPE-009 (no
constraint removal without origin investigation
— including no acceptance of an architectural
gap without investigation) make confirm-4-parent
the mechanical outcome. No Bill business
judgment is required.

3.5 F-12 — DIAGRAM D2 R2 G3 + G6 CELL-PROSE UPDATE
------------------------------------------------------------

WHAT THE FINDING IS.

Two cells in the per-pair pipeline diagram (D2
revision 2) contain prose text that has fallen
behind the project's current architecture:

CELL G3 currently encodes:
  "TRENDING_NEG (any vol) → STOP"

This reflects the pre-Stage-M-3 framing where
trending-negative regimes blocked all trade
activity. The project has since adopted the
directional-cascade framework (Hard Rule
HR-REGIME-007). Under directional cascade:
TRENDING_NEG blocks LONG entries but PERMITS
SHORT entries via the Short Module. The
companion diagram D4 R3 was updated when the
amendment was ratified. D2 R2 was not. The
diagrams are currently inconsistent with each
other.

CELL G6 currently consumes the parameter
"per_trade_pct." That parameter was retired at
TB00000 v2_29. The current v2_29 §9.17
parameter registry replaced it with two
CIATS-owned parameters: per_trade_size_usd
(starting at Kraken's per-pair minimum, about
$0.50) and per_trade_ceiling_pct (starting at
25% of the module wallet). G6 still references
the retired name.

OPTION (RATIFY specific wording) — Adopt
specific replacement text for both cells that
matches the current canonical sources.

OPTION (REVISE) — Bill specifies alternate
wording.

ANALYSIS.

Criterion (i) diagrams-canonical: the goal of
this finding IS to restore the diagram's truth-
telling. Either option meets the criterion in
principle; the question is which wording is
correct.

Criterion (ii) AA=08 over AA=10: G3 wording must
match HR-REGIME-007 (defined in 0711002 dv1_1,
the AA=07 Regime Taxonomy spec). G6 wording
must match v2_29 §9.17 (the canonical parameter
registry in TB00000). Both canonical sources
exist and are unambiguous.

Criterion (iii) loss prevention: leaving the
stale wording in place means the diagram lies
about how the bot handles trending-negative
regimes (it does NOT stop trading; it stops
LONG trading and permits SHORT trading via the
Short Module). A developer or auditor relying
on the stale wording could mis-implement a
loss-prevention boundary. The retired-parameter
reference could similarly cause confusion in
sizing-curve discussions. Fix is loss-prevention
positive.

Criterion (iv) information richness: the
directional-cascade wording carries more
information than the blanket "STOP" wording (it
names both directions and the Short Module
dispatch). The current-parameter wording names
the actual CIATS-owned controls instead of a
retired single parameter. More information in
both cases.

Criterion (v) rework cost: cell-text strings
only. SVG visual elements unchanged. Standard
diagram-amendment cost (one cell text edit per
cell, two cells total).

RECOMMENDATION.

Ratify specific wording. The replacements:

  G3 cell — replace
    "TRENDING_NEG (any vol) → STOP"
  with
    "TRENDING_NEG: LONG blocked; SHORT permitted
    via mod:Short_Module (HR-REGIME-007
    directional cascade)."

  G6 cell — replace the "consumes:
    param:per_trade_pct" reference with
    "consumes: param:per_trade_size_usd +
    param:per_trade_ceiling_pct (v2_29 §9.17
    R:R-driven sizing curve)."

Both replacements are derived directly from the
current canonical sources (HR-REGIME-007 in
0711002 dv1_1; v2_29 §9.17 in TB00000) without
new design content. The amendment is included
in AMENDMENT-15 in the close-gate package.

WHY THIS DECISION IS DERIVABLE WITHOUT BILL
JUDGMENT.

Both replacement strings are the mechanical
output of substituting current canonical content
for retired content. No new design content is
introduced. RR-SCOPE-005 (derivable-decision
execution) applies — surface as ratification
not as revision question.

3.6 F-16 — PLURAL-ALERT PROPAGATION GAP
------------------------------------------------------------

WHAT THE FINDING IS.

The bot sends alerts (drawdown halts, WS failures,
emergency stops, etc.) by email. There are two
candidate destination addresses:

  - "alert@tothbot.com" (singular) — does not
    exist at the mail provider. Proton Mail's
    domain catch-all reserves the singular form
    so the mailbox cannot be created. Every
    email sent here bounces with "550 5.1.1
    Recipient address rejected."

  - "alerts@tothbot.com" (plural) — exists.
    Emails sent here are delivered.

In April 2026 (TB00115) Bill discovered that
between 04-19 and 04-20 every CRITICAL/HIGH
alert had bounced — the only signal that
anything was wrong was a bounce notification.
The fix (OI-019) renamed every code default
from singular to plural. The 1011013 dv1_5
amendment ratified that change at the VPS
Deployment coding-spec layer.

The Operations Infrastructure parent spec
(0211002) still has four places that route
alerts to the singular name:

  - §5 IN-NET-001 (network failure alert)
  - §7 IN-ALT-001 (alert routing rule)
  - §7 IN-ALT-003 (alert escalation rule)
  - §8 IN-LOG-003 (alert log entry)

The parent spec predates the OI-019 fix and was
never refreshed.

OPTION (AUTHORIZE PROSE-UPDATE-BR(a)) — change
all four references from singular to plural.

ANALYSIS.

Criterion (i) diagrams-canonical: the
architecture diagram doesn't directly encode
the alert recipient string; it references the
operational spec. Update the operational spec
and the diagram's truth-telling improves
indirectly.

Criterion (ii) AA=08 over AA=10: this is an
AA=02 parent spec versus an AA=10 child spec
question, not AA=08-vs-AA=10. Under normal
precedence the AA=02 parent would govern — but
in this case the AA=10 child carries the
ratified fix (OI-019), and the parent has
fallen behind. The AA=10 fix has documented
empirical justification (bounce evidence) and
explicit operator ratification. The AA=02
parent needs to be brought current — not
inverted.

Criterion (iii) loss prevention: the singular
mailbox bounces. Alerts to a bouncing mailbox
are invisible alerts. Invisible alerts mean
the operator cannot intervene during a crisis.
This is a direct, active, ongoing loss-
prevention failure for the part of 0211002
that still references singular. Fix is
urgent.

Criterion (iv) information richness: zero
impact on information content; this is purely
a routing-correctness issue.

Criterion (v) rework cost: four mechanical
string replacements in one document. RR-SCOPE-
005 derivable (the answer is unambiguous —
plural is the only one that works).

Five criteria. Three favor fix unambiguously
(i, iii, v). Criterion (ii) has a wrinkle that
resolves toward fix anyway. Criterion (iv) is
a tie.

RECOMMENDATION.

Authorize PROSE-UPDATE-BR(a). Amend 0211002
in all four places (IN-NET-001, IN-ALT-001,
IN-ALT-003, IN-LOG-003) to replace
"alert@tothbot.com" with "alerts@tothbot.com."

The amendment is bundled into AMENDMENT-16 in
the close-gate package.

WHY THIS DECISION IS DERIVABLE WITHOUT BILL
JUDGMENT.

The empirical evidence (singular mailbox does
not exist; alerts bounce) plus the existing
ratified fix at the AA=10 layer plus the active
loss-prevention failure make this a confirmation,
not a decision. Per RR-SCOPE-005, surface as
recommended authorization rather than as
question for Bill to decide.

3.8 F-24 — VPS DEPLOYMENT READY-CRITERION
            AMENDMENT
------------------------------------------------------------

WHAT THE FINDING IS.

When the bot starts up on the VPS, it tells the
operating system "I am READY" via the sd_notify
mechanism. The condition under which the bot
declares itself READY is defined in the VPS
Deployment coding spec (1011013 dv1_6 §7 rule
VD-WD-004) as four sub-conditions, all of
which must be met:

  (a) All WS channels subscribed.
  (b) All pairs in READY state.
  (c) Position Mirror reconciled.
  (d) Kraken Status API check complete.

Two of the four sub-conditions are wrong.

PROBLEM WITH (b) — "All pairs in READY state."

Three canonical sources unanimously specify "at
least one pair in READY state" as the trigger:

  - 1011014 dv1_8 (Startup_Sequence coding
    spec) SS-STARTUP-022/026.
  - 0211003 dv1_0 (Startup/Shutdown procedure)
    SS-START-009.
  - 0500000 dv1_46 (architecture diagram)
    AR-049 step (9).

"All pairs in READY state" is operationally
infeasible. Pairs can remain in BLOCKED state
(failed indicator warmup, insufficient
liquidity, etc.) or in WARM_UP state
(insufficient candle history) indefinitely.
Waiting for ALL pairs to become READY would
mean the bot might NEVER become READY — even
when many pairs are perfectly tradeable. This
defeats the entire first-READY-pair startup
design that the three canonical sources
encode.

PROBLEM WITH (c) — "Position Mirror reconciled."

This sub-condition is correct in live mode.
Position Mirror reconciliation happens at
startup Step 6 (snap_orders) and ensures the
bot's view of open positions matches Kraken's
view. But in paper mode, Step 6 is skipped per
SS-PAPER-004 (paper mode does not connect to
the private WebSocket; there are no real
Kraken positions to reconcile). Without a
paper-mode exception clause, paper mode could
never satisfy sub-condition (c) and could
never become READY.

OPTION (RATIFY CA-1 (b) wording change AND
CA-2 (c) paper-mode exception clause).

ANALYSIS.

Criterion (i) diagrams-canonical: the
architecture diagram already specifies "at
least one pair READY" via AR-049 step (9).
Sub-condition (b) currently contradicts the
diagram. CA-1 brings the coding spec back into
agreement with the diagram. CA-2 makes the
paper-mode behavior explicit; the diagram
already encodes paper-mode skipping of Step 6
elsewhere (paper-mode-canonical principle from
TB00000 §4.4). Both fixes restore diagram-
canonical agreement.

Criterion (ii) AA=08 over AA=10: 1011013 is
an AA=10 coding spec. The three canonical
sources include the AA=08 architectural
position (via 0500000 diagram) and the AA=02
operational procedure (0211003). Both senior
layers say "at least one pair." CA-1 brings
the AA=10 spec into compliance with the
senior layers.

Criterion (iii) loss prevention: a bot that
never becomes READY cannot trade. Cannot
trade means cannot make money — but also
means cannot LOSE money via active trading.
However, a bot that fails to become READY is
also a bot that produces ZERO data for CIATS,
ZERO trade observations, ZERO regime-engine
validation, and a frustrated operator who may
work around the issue in unsafe ways. The
correct behavior (start trading once SOMETHING
is ready) is loss-prevention positive over
the project lifetime. CA-2 prevents paper-
mode total stall, which has its own data-loss
costs (paper-trading IS the master per §4.4
PA-001; if paper can't run, the project
can't make progress).

Criterion (iv) information richness: not
directly applicable.

Criterion (v) rework cost: two mechanical
edits in one document. CA-1 changes one word
("All" → "At least one"). CA-2 adds a short
clause to sub-condition (c). Single-pass fix.

All applicable criteria favor RATIFY.

RECOMMENDATION.

Ratify CA-1 and CA-2 together.

CA-1 — Amend 1011013 §7 VD-WD-004 sub-condition
(b):

  Replace
    "All pairs in READY state."
  with
    "At least one pair in READY state."

CA-2 — Amend 1011013 §7 VD-WD-004 sub-condition
(c):

  Replace
    "Position Mirror reconciled."
  with
    "Position Mirror reconciled (skipped in
    paper mode per SS-PAPER-004)."

The amendments are bundled into AMENDMENT-17 in
the close-gate package.

WHY THIS DECISION IS DERIVABLE WITHOUT BILL
JUDGMENT.

The three canonical sources unanimously specify
the "at least one pair READY" form. The paper-
mode skip of Step 6 is already canonical
elsewhere. Both fixes are mechanical alignment
to existing canonical sources. No Bill business
judgment is needed. Per RR-SCOPE-005, surface
as recommended ratification.
