  Bill chat-turn 3 verbatim: "We are going to be doing

  AUTHORIZATION ORIGIN ROOT-CAUSE. The "no new diagrams"
  interpretation came from a sentence in the TB00212 NSI
  (authored by Claude at TB00211 close): "Existing 8
  figures stay byte-identical except for Gate 7 + Gate 8
  sub-diagrams receiving the two node additions." Claude
  read this self-authored phrase as forbidding new
  figures. Bill never authorized that interpretation.
  Self-citing chain failure: Claude treated its own
  prior-session output as authoritative direction. The
  right reading of that phrase: it scoped THIS specific
  1011011 amendment under an assumption that turned out
  to be wrong (the assumption being that Gate 7 and Gate
  8 had existing sub-diagrams to extend; they don't).

  FP/DP ANALYSIS EXECUTED across five evaluation
  dimensions:

    (1) CANONICAL FOR CODING. Today's 8 figures are
        architectural overview. Gate 7 is a single
        rectangle labeled "Drawdown halt / concentration
        / exposure." That tells a coder WHAT exists, not
        HOW to build it (computation order, data inputs,
        race conditions, error paths). When the 12
        retiring coding specs migrate, that "how" detail
        must live somewhere canonical. Sub-figures
        elaborating gate and module internals qualify.

    (2) CORRECTNESS FOR CODING. Without sub-figures,
        coders interpret the high-level boxes.
        Interpretation introduces variance. Sub-figures
        pin behavior down.

    (3) CLARITY FOR CODING. The discarded inline-diamond
        approach put a CHECK 5 diamond next to Gate 7
        with no CHECK 1-4 anywhere — worse than the
        previous single-rectangle hand-wave because it
        raised questions the diagram couldn't answer.

    (4) LOSS PREVENTION. Coder interpretation error
        introduces defects. Defects lose money. Per the
        primary mission (minimize loss), this is the
        dominant evaluation dimension.

    (5) DRIFT SURFACE. 12 retiring coding specs versus
        approximately 6-10 new sub-figures inside one
        parent document = NET DECREASE in places that
        can drift out of sync. Sub-figures live in one
        document; today's 13 separately-versioned
        coding specs are 13 separately-versioned
        documents.

  All five dimensions pointed the same direction.
  Outcome: NEW SUB-FIGURES needed, on a per-audit basis,
  added when the existing figures genuinely cannot
  accommodate the unique architectural content being
  migrated from the retiring spec.
