3.1 CORE MISSION RESTATEMENT.

Verbatim Bill: "I speak human, not computer. We were
supposed to be completing the diagrams so they were
the complete and canonical, authoritative source for
TothBot for coding. Prose supplement diagrams. We were
supposed to be streamlining the documents and removing
any redundant prose and any 10XXXXX documents that
were redundant and streamlining/reducing those that
were necessary."

Interpretation:

(a) DIAGRAMS-CANONICAL REAFFIRMED.
    The principle that the diagrams in 0500000
    (the System Architecture Overview) are the
    authoritative source — and that prose contextualizes
    but does not govern — has been in force since
    TB00164 (TB00000 v2_22 → v2_23 §4.5 enactment).
    Every amendment, prose update, and streamlining
    action must reinforce this principle.

(b) STREAMLINING DIRECTIVE NEW.
    Three workstreams added to Step 3 scope:
    (i)   Remove redundant prose from existing
          documents (prose that duplicates what the
          diagram already says).
    (ii)  Identify 10XXXXX coding specs that
          duplicate other coding specs and remove
          them entirely.
    (iii) Reduce remaining 10XXXXX specs to the
          minimum content necessary for Claude to
          code from.

(c) PLAIN-LANGUAGE FRAMING.
    All communications, deliverables, and analyses
    must be readable in plain English without
    governance-jargon decoding. This restates the
    plain-language discipline already codified in
    TB00000 v2_28 §4.6 (which was enacted three
    sessions ago, TB00173, after three recurrences
    under lesson-text-only governance). Bill chat-
    turn 4 confirms the pattern recurred again in
    TB00207 chat-turn 3.

3.2 AMENDMENT-17 EXPLANATION GAP.

Verbatim Bill: "AMENDMENT-17: 0211003 Startup/Shutdown
— 75 token additions (LARGEST single-spec) - I don't
understand the 75 tokens. We are trading all
USD/USDC/USDT Token pairs."

Interpretation: the "75 tokens" in the close-gate
package §2 AMENDMENT-17 entry was a count of governance
identifier references — NOT trading pairs. The package
language was opaque and gave Bill no usable information
about what the Startup/Shutdown spec is actually
missing. TB00208 produces a plain-language replacement
explanation describing what 0211003 needs in terms of
operational behavior (startup sequence steps, shutdown
sequence steps, paper-mode awareness, drawdown halt
hooks), not in terms of identifier counts.

3.3 GATE 2 — FP/DP ANALYSIS REQUIRED.

Verbatim Bill: "Do a FP/DP analysis, in the next chat
session to see what option produces the least errors
and rework."

Interpretation: TB00208 produces a First-Principles /
Data-Point analysis comparing the three options for

rework. Output: definitive recommendation with FP/DP
justification — not three options for Bill to choose
between.

3.4 GATE 3 — FP/DP ANALYSIS + ONE-DOCUMENT-PER-TURN
    RULE.

Verbatim Bill: "Again, I don't speak computer. Do the
all in the most efficient order to produces the least
errors and rework. Use as many chat sessions as
necessary and produce only one document per turn so
you don't lose any if you crap out. Do a FP/DP
analysis to determine the best method to produces the
least errors and rework."

Interpretation:

(a) GATE 3 FP/DP analysis required in TB00208.
    Decision criterion: least errors and rework.

(b) ONE-DOCUMENT-PER-TURN RULE established.
    Going forward, every chat-response-turn produces
    exactly ONE document deliverable. Prevents
    multi-document loss if a chat fails mid-turn.
    Session-close trio (Session Log + Next Session
    Instructions + OCS copy box) remains the only
    exception — three pieces but one logical session-
    close action.

(c) USE AS MANY CHAT SESSIONS AS NECESSARY.
    Chat-overload risk takes priority over session-
    count minimization. Better 30 lean chats than 5
    overloaded chats that fail.
