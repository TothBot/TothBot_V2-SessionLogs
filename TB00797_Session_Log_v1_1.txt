   He challenged my claim that retail can't have multiple wallets/subaccounts. Two more research
   agents confirmed he was RIGHT and I was wrong:

      DERIVATIVES subaccounts are open to "any eligible client" - own balance/margin/liquidation/
      API keys/rate limits; one sub's liquidation cannot touch another's (true ring-fence).
    - The "nephew" memory = a separate FULL account (Kraken permits multiple full accounts and
      NAMES "conflicting strategies / multiple bots / separating margin" as legitimate uses).
 - MAPPING: Long Spot = its own full account (confirmed). Long Perp + Short Perp = two LINKED
   DERIVATIVES SUBACCOUNTS (the US Derivatives Client Agreement FORBIDS multiple UNLINKED derivatives
   accounts - they MUST be a subaccount structure, so subaccounts are the prescribed, not workaround,
   mechanism). Fallback if US-perp retail subaccounts are not live: both perp sides in one Futures
   wallet under ISOLATED margin per position + CIATS accounting.
