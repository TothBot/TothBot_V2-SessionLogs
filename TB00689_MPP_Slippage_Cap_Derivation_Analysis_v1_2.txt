2. FIRST-PRINCIPLES CONSTRAINTS
------------------------------------------------------------
(a) LOSS-MIN ASYMMETRY (governing). Cap too TIGHT: IOC
    zero-fills, candidate dropped -- missed entry, zero
    capital loss. Cap too LOOSE: a degraded fill silently
    pushes the realized expected R:R below the sacred 1.5
    floor the candidate was admitted on. Bias TIGHT.
(b) FLOOR-PRESERVATION MATH (the 1:1.5-or-better estimation
    requirement, exact). Let E = expected_reward, N =
    net_loss, both fractions of the reference price, with
    E/N >= 1.5 at A1. A fill worse than reference by slippage
    fraction s gives, to first order:
      realized reward E' = E - s   (the run-to-reversal exit
        price is independent of entry; the favorable excursion
        shrinks by exactly the slippage)
      realized risk   N' ~= N     (mae_pct = ATR*mult/fill --
        a worse fill RAISES the denominator, shrinking mae_pct
        slightly; the approximation errs conservative; fee
        fractions unchanged)
    The realized ratio clears the floor iff
      (E - s) / N >= 1.5   <=>   s <= E - 1.5 * N.
    DEFINE  rr_headroom = expected_reward - 1.5 * net_loss.
    rr_headroom is the mathematically exact maximum slippage
    that preserves the 1:1.5-or-better estimate. It is derived
    directly from the hardcoded floor; it is NOT a parameter
    and is never tunable. Both inputs are already produced per
    candidate at D9 g8d (evt:G8_SIZED payload) -- zero new
    computation or data dependency.
(c) FILLABILITY. A marketable IOC must cross the spread plus
    any instantaneous price displacement between the candle
    close that triggered the pipeline and order arrival. The
    cap must cover the NORMAL magnitude of that displacement
    (or entries never fill) and exclude the abnormal tail
    (elevated/extreme spread -- the hazardous fills).
(d) NO LIVE SPREAD AT ENTRY. Pairs without open positions run
    ticker event_trigger=trades, not bbo (WS-TKR-002); no
    order-book channel is subscribed. Live-spread-based caps
    require new subscriptions -- architecture change, out of
    scope. Historical OHLC is the admissible data source
    (same ruling basis as TB00654).
(e) DETERMINISM + DECIMAL. Recipe deterministic per candidate,
    all-Decimal (AR-047 / HR-SP-008), quantization in the
    cap-tightening direction (canon, unchanged).

3. STATISTICAL DERIVATION OF THE SEED (historical OHLC)
------------------------------------------------------------
THE MEASURABLE QUANTITY. Entry triggers on a committed 5m
candle close; the order arrives moments into the next candle.
The OHLC-derivable proxy for the instantaneous adverse price
displacement the order must absorb is the CLOSE-TO-NEXT-OPEN
gap (crypto trades 24/7, so open(t+1) ~= first traded price
after close(t)):

  per committed candle t, per pair, per side:
    long side:   d_t = max(0, (open(t+1) - close(t)) / close(t))
    short side:  d_t = max(0, (close(t) - open(t+1)) / close(t))
  (only the ADVERSE direction is a cost; favorable gaps are
  clipped to zero, not netted -- conservative.)

THE STATISTIC. The empirical distribution of d_t is heavy-
tailed (gap distributions are leptokurtic; news/liquidity
spikes dominate the tail). A Gaussian fit (mean + k*sigma)
UNDERSTATES the tail and is statistically inadmissible here;
the NONPARAMETRIC EMPIRICAL QUANTILE is the appropriate
estimator -- no distributional assumption, robust to outliers
by construction.

  mpp_abs_cap_pct(pair, side) = Q95[ d_t ]
    = the empirical 95th-percentile adverse close-to-open
      displacement over the historical OHLC window.

WHY THE 95TH PERCENTILE (loss-min derivation, not taste):
  - By construction ~95% of normal-condition entries on that
    pair fill through the cap: the fill rate is a DERIVED,
    quantified property of the seed, not a hope.
  - The excluded ~5% tail is, by definition, the candles with
    abnormal instantaneous displacement -- news events, thin
    books, spread blowouts (the AR-078 elevated/extreme bands).
    Those are precisely the entries loss-min wants REFUSED;
    their refusal cost is opportunity only (constraint 2(a)).
  - A higher quantile (P99) admits tail fills (anti-loss-min);
    a lower quantile (P75/P50) rejects routine entries for no
    risk benefit. P95 is the knee implied by the asymmetry:
    pay nothing in capital to refuse the hazardous 5%.
  - SANITY ANCHOR against in-canon data: on $500k+ pairs the
    normal displacement scale is the normal-spread band
    (0.01-0.05%, canon 1(e)); Q95 per pair is expected to land
    near or just above that band and BELOW the elevated band
    (0.2-1.0%) -- the same separation the v1_0 fixed value
    targeted, now measured per pair instead of asserted.

DATASET + EXECUTION (mirrors TB00654 exactly): computed OFF
the hot path over the SAME historical OHLC dataset the
expected_reward_estimator_seed backtest replays, per pair in
the monitored universe (decision:D-04 floor), per side.
Per-regime refinement follows the 600-trade per-regime canon:
CIATS may split the statistic per regime once per-regime data
suffices; the seed is per pair/side. Insufficient-history
pairs (newly listed): fall back to the universe median Q95
until the pair accrues its own window -- never guessed.

4. RECOMMENDED RECIPE (boot state) -- RATIFIED
------------------------------------------------------------
Per candidate, at AR-069 limit-price construction, all
Decimal:

  rr_headroom  = expected_reward - (3/2) * net_loss
  mpp_cap_pct  = min( mpp_abs_cap_pct(pair, side) ,
                      rr_headroom )

  mpp_abs_cap_pct(pair, side)  [SEED] = empirical Q95 of the
      adverse close-to-next-open displacement from the
      historical OHLC backtest dataset (section 3).

  Long:  entry_limit_price = entry_ref_price * (1 + mpp_cap_pct)
         .quantize(price_increment, ROUND_DOWN)
  Short: entry_limit_price = entry_ref_price * (1 - mpp_cap_pct)
         .quantize(price_increment, ROUND_UP)

  mpp_cap_pct <= 0 -> limit = quantized reference (fills only
  if the book touches the reference; otherwise zero-fill ->
  candidate dropped per G5 disposition). No special-case path.

WHY THE min(): the two terms close each other's failure mode.
  - rr_headroom (exact math, section 2(b)) makes a floor-
    breaching fill IMPOSSIBLE: no admitted fill can have a
    first-order realized expectation below 1:1.5. This is the
    "best meet the 1:1.5 R:R or better entry point estimation"
    requirement, satisfied exactly, by derivation from the
    hardcoded floor itself.
  - Q95 (measured statistics, section 3) stops high-headroom
    candidates from paying tail prices into elevated-spread
    books: floor-preserving but value-destroying fills are
    refused.
  Each term alone fails loss-min in one direction; together
  neither failure mode exists.

WORKED EXAMPLE (typical liquid pair; illustrative Q95 = 0.06%;
5m ATR = 0.2% of price):
  mae_pct  = 1.5 * 0.20% = 0.30%
  net_loss = 0.30% + 0.26% + 0.26% = 0.82%
  Floor requires expected_reward >= 1.23%.
  Candidate A: E = 1.60% -> headroom = 0.37%
    -> cap = min(0.06%, 0.37%) = 0.06% (Q95 binds; fills
    through any normal-condition displacement on this pair).
  Candidate B: E = 1.30% -> headroom = 0.07%
    -> cap = 0.06% (Q95 still binds; fills normally).
  Candidate C: E = 1.26% -> headroom = 0.03%
    -> cap = 0.03% (headroom binds; fills only on a tight
    book -- a marginal candidate cannot afford a degraded
    fill; zero-fills otherwise. Correct by 2(b)).
  Candidate D: E = 1.23% (at floor) -> cap ~ 0 -> limit =
    reference -> effectively zero-fills. Correct by 2(b).

KNOWN PROXY LIMITATION (stated, not hidden): the close-to-open
gap embeds trade-price bounce but not the standing half-spread
itself; the proxy can understate true crossing cost by up to
~half the spread (0.005-0.025% on the monitored universe).
This is a SEED-quality limitation only: from the first fills,
CIATS observes ACTUAL realized slippage (WS-EXE-012 last_price
/ avg_price vs entry_ref_price) and corrects -- the same
seed-then-correct structure DEC-124 ratified for the
expected-reward estimate.

CIATS OWNERSHIP AND CONTROL (per Bill ruling, v1_2): mpp_abs_
cap_pct is CIATS-OWNED FROM THE START. The historical-OHLC Q95
derivation IS CIATS setting the seed from data -- a large
per-pair sample (thousands of candles), statistically valid on
day one; there is nothing to wait for to OWN the value. The
200-trade floor gates LIVE-DATA TUNING ONLY -- the point below
which the live paper sample is too small to adjust against
without chasing noise -- never ownership. This mirrors the
DEC-124 expected_reward_estimator_seed exactly: CIATS-owned,
historical seed, tuned from paper data from the 200-trade
floor. So:
  - from trade 1: CIATS OWNS the value, set from historical
    data (the Q95 derivation, section 3);
  - from the 200-trade floor: CIATS REFINES it against live
    realized slippage and zero-fill rate.
CIATS tunes it on two existing observables:
  - rejection_rate_by_regime (AR-069 IOC zero-fill tracking):
    elevated zero-fill rate -> cap costing entries -> propose
    quantile/value increase;
  - realized fill slippage (WS-EXE-012 analytics): realized
    slippage distribution hugging the cap -> cap binding and
    being paid -> propose decrease.
  Statistical machinery: the canon CIATS methods apply
  directly -- EWMA (lambda=0.2) on realized slippage and
  zero-fill rate; CUSUM for distribution-shift detection
  (a structural spread change on a pair); Mann-Whitney U for
  paper-vs-seed distribution comparison at the 200-trade
  floor. Changes flow through normal CIATS PDCA proposals.
The rr_headroom TERM is NOT a parameter and is never CIATS-
tunable: it is the sacred 1.5 floor expressed at the execution
boundary. CIATS may never propose a cap construction admitting
a fill whose first-order realized expectation is below the
floor (direct corollary of the A1 sacred constraint).

4a. EXPECTED-VALUE (EV) CONSIDERATION -- REJECTED AT THE CAP
------------------------------------------------------------
Bill asked whether a poker-style EV calculation
(p_win * reward - p_loss * loss) should enter the cap recipe.
RULING (this session): NO -- redundant at the cap, and at the
cap a pure EV-max would push AGAINST loss-min. Three grounds:

  (1) The EV protection is already present. The sacred net
      1:1.5 R:R floor is the project's deliberately-
      conservative EV proxy (a margin above breakeven). The
      rr_headroom term (section 2(b)) already guarantees that
      no fill drags a trade below that floor. A separate EV
      calc re-derives the same guarantee through a noisier
      path -- it would require a win-probability estimate the
      cap decision does not otherwise need.
  (2) The EV take/don't-take call is UPSTREAM. By the time the
      cap is constructed, A1 (D9 g8d) has already decided the
      trade is worth taking. The cap is not take/don't-take;
      it is "the worst fill that keeps an already-approved
      trade approved." EV belongs to the admission decision,
      which is settled at A1.
  (3) At the cap, EV-max FIGHTS loss-min. A pure EV-max values
      the skipped trade and would LOOSEN the cap to recover
      missed-fill opportunity. The governing asymmetry
      (2(a)) is the opposite: a missed fill costs zero, a bad
      fill costs real money -- bias TIGHT. EV here either
      reproduces the tight answer (redundant) or pushes looser
      (wrong by priority).

WHERE EV EARNS ITS KEEP (flagged, NOT this session): the CIATS
SIZING CURVE ABOVE THE FLOOR (per_trade_size_usd as a function
of edge from the 200-trade floor, TB00000 sec 8). The 1.5
floor is a RATIO and carries no win probability; two floor-
passing candidates can have very different true EV. A p(win)-
weighted EV is the correct quantity to size against, and it is
the missing input that makes the already-listed Half-Kelly
method (sec 5) operable (Kelly fraction = edge/odds; Half-Kelly
under-sizes for drawdown control -- consistent with loss-min).
Building it requires a per-candidate win-probability estimate
(data-gated: 200-trade floor for inference, 600 per regime;
the DEC-124 historical-OHLC backtest can seed p(win) the same
way it seeds expected reward) plus a sizing-curve diagram
element. This is a SEPARATE element, a SEPARATE session. No
action here beyond this flag: the sizing work makes allocation
a function of p(win)-weighted EV, not the R:R ratio, with
Half-Kelly as the consuming method.
