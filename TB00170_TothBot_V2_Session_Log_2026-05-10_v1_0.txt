    FIRST-PRINCIPLES ANALYSIS:
      1. DD-001: diagrams are canonical and authoritative;
         prose follows.
      2. Tier 1 is a canonical diagram; D1-D7 are canonical
         diagrams.
      3. drill_targets are pointers from Tier 1 to Tier 2.
      4. Pointers must reference STABLE identifiers, else
         the pointer drifts as the target evolves.
      5. R-states are MUTABLE versioning attributes
         (advance R-1 -> R-2 -> R-3). Canonical-name
         identity (DCN + Image[N] + Tier + ShortTitle)
         is IMMUTABLE.
      6. Therefore drill_targets should reference bare
         canonical-name (Image[N]_T2_[ShortTitle]), not
         R-state.

    DATA-DRIVEN ANALYSIS:
      Current state at dv1_36:
        D1=R-2, D2=R-1, D3=R-3, D4=R-1, D5=R-3, D6=R-3,
        D7=R-1.
        Tier 1 drill_targets: all _R1 form.
        Stale-as-R-state: 4 of 7 (D1, D3, D5, D6).
      Maintenance burden:
        "_R[R] = current R-state": 7 Tier 1 edits per
          stage cycle (Tier 1 R-increment forced on every
          Tier 2 R-increment).
        "_R1 = canonical-name component frozen at
          baseline": non-obvious convention, ambiguity
          for future readers, anomaly persists forever.
        "Bare canonical-name (no suffix)": single source
          of truth via DCN; Tier 1 stable across R-
          increments; convention obvious and self-
          explanatory.

    ORIGIN DETERMINATION:
      Tier 1 was authored at dv1_24 baseline when all
      D-figures were at R-1. The _R1 suffix in dv1_24-
      era drill_targets was indistinguishable from
      canonical-name (single-state world). DD-001 and

      T1-B contract; v1_7 codified T1-B requiring _R[R]
      form which was a reaffirmation of the dv1_24 form
      rather than a re-analysis. Confirmed: leftover from
      pre-canonical-paradigm authoring.

    CONCLUSION:
      Correct convention under DD-001 = BARE CANONICAL-
      NAME form [DCN]_Image[N]_T2_[ShortTitle], without
      R-state suffix.
