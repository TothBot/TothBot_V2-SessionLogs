============================================================
BATTERY C -- ISOLATED-MARGIN LIQUIDATION UNDER GAPS ... 7/7 PASS.
  The crash-proof loss cap is STRUCTURAL, not statistical: on isolated margin a position's realized POOL
  loss is bounded by the posted margin BY CONSTRUCTION, even on a gap-through to 50%+ (the overflow beyond
  margin is the EXCHANGE insurance fund's, not the pool's). Verified by gap sweeps, native-stop-fails (gap-
  through + a no-stop spec), every pair's worst REAL historical wick, a 40k-draw fat-tailed MC (99.9th-pct
  AND abs-max loss <= margin), and an all-pairs simultaneous-crash cascade. The 3 pools are BYTE-isolated
  (crash one -> others bit-for-bit identical = ZERO contagion = the sec-13.7 ring-fence). ONE design
  constraint (C7): keep leverage LOW (2-3x) so liquidation sits BELOW the 3xATR stop and stays a tail
  backstop; lev 2-3 preserves the edge byte-for-byte vs the no-margin baseline, lev>=5 liquidation eats the
  edge, lev 20 goes NEGATIVE. CAVEAT: real Kraken/Bitnomial maint-ratio + contract-mult are NON-PUBLIC --
  swept here, must be pinned from the rulebook at code time.

BATTERY A -- CONCURRENT TWO-POOL HEDGE DRAWDOWN ... 7/8 (the headline, honestly mixed).
  The sec-13.7 ring-fenced hedge is DRAWDOWN / REGIME INSURANCE, NOT a risk-adjusted-return improver.
  HOLDS: at EQUAL capital the combined 3-pool book cuts maxDD ~50% vs long-only ($620 vs $1226); Long vs
  Short monthly PnL anti-correlated (-0.10 overall, ~0 bear); shuffled MC tail tighter; worst month ~halved
  (-$175 vs -$452); maxDD lower in 6/6 walk-forward eras incl all 3 bear eras; the no-same-instrument
  collision rule is FREE (+5.6%, it removes trades fighting an open position). FAILS (honest, pre-registered,
  kept visible): the combined full-sample CALMAR does NOT beat the pure spot long (13.6 vs 16.1) -- the spot
  long is the single best risk-adjusted pool; every perp allocation trades some return for safety. => This
  CONFIRMS 'size the short as a HEDGE, not a profit center' (perps-revalidation-verdict) and the cycle
  finding that the win shows up in the WORST phase not the full-sample average (cycle-strategy-verdict). The
  A6 allocation frontier is the CIATS size knob: a light 90/5/5 split keeps ~100% of long-only Calmar while
  buying the tail insurance; heavier short weight buys more drawdown protection at a Calmar cost.

BATTERY B -- ADVERSE / SUSTAINED-FUNDING STRESS ... 4/5 (one genuine sensitivity).
  Funding is largely SECOND-ORDER and de-risked: in the historical positive-funding regime the short
  COLLECTS funding on average (a tailwind), so real or 5x-amplified funding HELPS it (B1); worst single real
  funding cost ~$5 vs the $16.67 margin (B3); funding cost NOT compounded with price losses (corr -0.04, B4);
  the bear-hedge role survives 2x funding (B5). THE ONE EXCEPTION (B2, honest FAIL): because the short is a
  THIN ~2.5% edge, funding pinned hard-adverse SUSTAINED across the whole holding history erases it --
  break-even ~0.13%/day (0.04%/8h), ABOVE typical funding (4x headroom) but BELOW the exchange clamp ceiling
  (0.225%/day). A permanent pin is unphysical, but it flags sustained-adverse funding as the short's most
  material cost -> a CIATS MONITORING knob (trim the short on a sharp sustained funding flip), folding into
  the 0500000 sec-13.6 tier-divergence monitor. Extends the TB00805a fee-robustness finding: neither per-
  contract fee NOR typical funding is the lever; the edge is native short ACCESS + the rsi_trend/vol/wide-
  stop config -- but funding is the one cost worth a live monitor.
