THE DEFECT BILL IDENTIFIED
  While reviewing the fee/R:R surfaces, Bill flagged that the
  live V2 take-profit model CAPS winning positions: a fixed
  resting post_only TP limit is placed at entry at gross_target
  = net_gain (= net_loss x 1.5), so a winner auto-sells at
  exactly 1.5x risk; plus max_hold_candles = 24 forces a 2-hour
  TIME_EXPIRY exit. Bill stated this contradicts the original
  TothBot design (run-to-reversal, no fixed target, no max
  hold), that he did not authorize the change, and directed that
  the "when did it change" question not be pursued.

ARCHIVE TRACE (public github.com/TothBot/TothBot-Docs, HEAD)
  0741001_Exit_Controller_Module_Spec_dv1_3 -- the original
  design EXPLICITLY analyzed and rejected BOTH caps V2 later
  reintroduced:
    section 3.3 "Why No Separate Take Profit Rule": a fixed TP
      "caps the upside of winning positions at an arbitrary
      price level"; a position at target may still hold edge
      (target is not evidence, the reversal signal is); a
      10,000-trial FP/DP simulation (2026-03-17) of run-to-
      reversal (Method A) vs fixed TP (Method C) gave higher
      average win + profit factor for run-to-reversal, loss
      metrics within noise, and LOWER max portfolio drawdown
      (1.64% vs 1.97%); the reversal signal IS the take-profit
      mechanism (one exit rule governs all).
    section 3.4 "Why No Maximum Hold Time": a time limit
      "would systematically cut winning positions that have
      sustained edge ... directly conflicts with the profit
      maximization mission"; CIATS monitors hold-time
      distributions instead of a hard limit.
  The original used the reversal detector (DPS reversal) as the
  sole take-profit / time governor. min R:R existed (PARAM-006,
  0731002) as a TUNABLE entry-acceptance parameter, NOT a fixed
  exit target. No authorization record for the V2 reintroduction
  of the caps was sought (per Bill direction).

BILL RULING -- DEC-124 (RATIFIED IN CHAT; AUTHORITATIVE per
TB00000 section 4.1 chat-turn directive)
  1. The sacred net 1:1.5 R:R minimum STAYS sacred and hardcoded
     but is the ENTRY-ACCEPTANCE FLOOR ONLY -- a minimum-expected-
     value admission test on expected R:R at entry. It NEVER
     closes a position. It is NOT an exit cap.
  2. RUN-TO-REVERSAL is the exit point for the WHOLE position.
     No fixed gross_target TP limit; no max-hold time cap. V2's
     regime-reversal detectors (HTF_REGIME_REVERSAL /
     DAILY_REGIME_DOWNGRADE) carry the original DPS-reversal
     role; protective exits (MAE Layer 2, emergency stop Layer 3)
     are retained for the loss side.
  3. The expected-reward input to the 1.5 entry floor is a CIATS-
     owned estimate of run-to-reversal size, seeded pre-200-
     trades and refined from paper-trading data after the floor.
     Data-driven, never guessed -- the explicit purpose of the
     original analysis.
  4. EV FRAME (Bill, governing rationale): the decision is an
     expected-value computation over the outcome DISTRIBUTION,
     not per-trade. Some individual trades give back more, but
     the original 10,000-trial sim showed the net delta is
     positive (higher average win + profit factor) AND lower max
     drawdown -- +EV on both the profit tail and the loss tail.
     The 1.5 floor is the minimum-EV entry admission test (the
     "fold" on sub-1.5 setups); run-to-reversal is the +EV
     harvest (no right-tail truncation); CIATS is the EV
     estimator that sharpens with data.
