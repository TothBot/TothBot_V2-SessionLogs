CATEGORY C: SINGLE NUMERIC VALUE CHANGE (dv1_24 -> dv1_25).

  MAX_RECONNECT_ATTEMPTS: 20 (dv1_24) -> 10 (dv1_25 spec
  WM-RECONNECT-017).

  Q1 — When introduced as 20?
    TB00105 (TB00000 v2_10 entry) raised
    MAX_RECONNECT_ATTEMPTS 10 -> 20 as transient bridge
    over Kraken EAPI:Invalid-key outage and OI-018
    WS_MGR_FATAL pattern. Bridge value carried forward
    in dv1_24 deployed code.

  Q2 — What problem was it intended to solve?
    Riding out back-to-back Kraken transient API
    outages without a fatal cascade -> systemd-restart
    -> full subscribe storm. At the time, single-
    connection architecture meant fatal-reconnect
    pulled the entire universe through a fresh
    subscribe storm.

  Q3 — Evidence the original problem is gone OR the
    constraint is not the correct fix?
    OI-018, OI-020, OI-041 closed at TB00125 deploy.
    Transient outage class no longer dominant.
    PATH 2 sharding (HR-WM-025): per-shard reconnect
    bounds blast radius to one shard's symbol partition;
    a shard going FATAL does not pull other shards down.
    Token bucket (HR-WM-032): even if fatal-reconnect
    occurs and systemd restarts the process, subscribe
    rate is bucket-bounded.
    1011002 dv1_25 spec WM-RECONNECT-017 explicitly
    documents 10 attempts as the contract value
    (worst-case wall time 181s).

  Q4 — Loss-prevention property preserved by some other
    mechanism after change?
    Yes:
      (a) Per-shard reconnect independence (HR-WM-025)
          confines fatal-reconnect to one shard.
      (b) emergSL resting orders protect open positions
          during fatal-reconnect -> systemd restart
          window (live mode; paper has no positions on
          Kraken book).
      (c) systemd Restart=on-failure + RestartSec=5
          (1011013 dv1_4) auto-restarts after a fatal
          cascade.
      (d) systemd StartLimitBurst=3 +
          StartLimitIntervalSec=600 (1011013 dv1_6
          VD-SYS-008) caps restart cadence.

  Decision: spec-conforming change. dv1_25 implements
  the contract value (10). Loss-prevention property
  preserved in full by mechanisms (a)-(d) above.

CATEGORY D: STRUCTURAL SHAPE CHANGES (spec-conforming
  refactors required by PATH 2 contract).

  Replacement of single self._ws_public connection by
    self.shards: List[WSShard]. Required by
    WM-SHARD-003.
  Replacement of single _recv_loop_public /
    _ping_loop / _zombie_monitor by per-shard variants.
    Required by HR-WM-025 / HR-WM-029.
  Addition of pair_to_shard_index. Required by
    WM-SHARD-002.
  Addition of pair_states dict per shard. Required
    by WM-SHARD-010.
  Renaming monitored_universe -> tradeable_universe.
    Aligns 1011002 dv1_25 with 0511001 dv1_4 §5.1
    canonical naming (terminology fix only; the
    underlying set semantics are the same).

  None remove a loss-prevention constraint. All
  required by the spec contract.

NO PROHIBITED REMOVALS. RR-SCOPE-009 self-catch: PASS.
