1.1 WHAT TO AUTHOR
    The SIGNAL_DECAY detection condition: the signal-model rule
    that determines WHEN a still-working (unfilled or partially
    unfilled) entry order's originating signal has decayed such
    that the pending entry must be canceled. This is the
    DETECTION half (0711001 SSS_Signal_Model). The cancel-DISPATCH
    mechanics (0511003 Execution_Engine) and the cancel-CONTROL
    decision ownership (Selection Controller) are SEPARATE targets
    - see sec 2.2; do not conflate.

1.2 OWNERSHIP MODEL (Bill-ratified, do NOT re-derive)
    A single working entry order is owned by the Selection module
    (entry-side control) while unfilled. On a PARTIAL fill the one
    order BISECTS into two concurrently-owned objects:
      - filled qty  -> a real position, owned by the Exit
        Controller (AR-054 handles the unprotected-stub case).
      - unfilled remainder -> stays owned by the Selection module
        (entry-side).
    If SIGNAL_DECAY fires before the remainder fills, the
    SELECTION module (mod:Selection_Controller) cancels the
    unfilled remainder. This cancel is a SELECTION-module action,
    NOT an Exit-module / AR-054 action. AR-054 only handles the
    already-filled portion. The boundary is the fill: filled ->
    Exit; unfilled -> Selection.
