2.2 FP/DP Operations and CIATS Analysis
------------------------------------------------------------

Deep dive analysis of TothBot Operations and CIATS using
statistical first principles.

VALIDATED (no changes needed):
  EWMA λ=0.2: half-life = 3.1 evaluations. Correct for
    fast monitoring. Must not trigger parameter changes.
  200-trade floor: SE(win_rate) ≈ 3.5%, SE(Sharpe) ≈ 7.2%.
    Adequate for inference. Floor confirmed correct.
  600-trade regime floor: ~100/regime. Mann-Whitney power
    ≈ 85% at α=0.01. Floor confirmed correct.
  Half-Kelly: 75% expected growth at 75% variance reduction.
    Optimal tradeoff for loss-minimization mission.

GAPS FOUND AND RESOLVED IN WD DOCUMENTS:
  Mann-Whitney U significance level α not specified.
    FP recommendation: α=0.01 (one-sided). Rationale: 99%
    confidence required for financial parameter decisions.
    α=0.05 produces false positives that increase over-
    optimization risk. Captured in 1011010 Section 3.2.

  Spearman correlation thresholds not specified.
    FP recommendation: |ρ|>0.3 WITH p<0.05 (both conditions).
    Values below either threshold are treated as noise.
    Captured in 1011010 Section 3.3.

  CUSUM k and h parameters not specified anywhere.
    FP recommendation: k = 0.5σ of monitored metric,
    h = 4σ of monitored metric. Starting values subject
    to CIATS optimization. Captured in 1011010 Section 3.4.

  PDCA minimum change interval not specified.
    FP recommendation: minimum 50 trades between any two
    CIATS parameter changes (post-200 trade floor).
    Prevents over-optimization and ensures statistical
    independence between evaluation windows.
    Captured in 1011010 Section 3.5.

  Logger async queue size not specified.
    FP recommendation: 10,000 entries.
    Rationale: 20 positions × 10 events/candle = 200/candle.
    10,000 = 50-candle absorption buffer.
    Captured in 1011002 and 0311002 Step 2.13 note.

  Trade channel (PL-006) not evaluated.
    Evaluation conducted: NOT needed for V1. OHLC adequate
    at 5-minute frequency. Captured in 1011010 Section 3.6.
    PL-006 CLOSED.
